EXECUTIVE SUMMARY  3  The Office of Internal Audit and Investigations (OIAI) conducted an audit of   institutional service  contract s ,   covering the period from   1   January 2022 to   31   October   2023.   The audit   was conducted  between   1   March a nd   3 1 December   2023   in   conformance   with   the Code of Ethics and   the  International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing.   The   overarching  objective of the audit was to   assess the adequacy and effectiveness of the   governance, risk  management and control processes   over   a selection of   significant   risk areas related to UNICEF’s  institutional service contract s.   The descriptions of the specific risks   evaluated   are provided in the  Audit Objective s ,   Scope   and Approach   s ection of this report.  UNICEF   contracts   with   institutions   or   corporate   entities   to   procure   essential   services   at  h eadquarters, regional and country office levels to enable UNICEF to achieve its goals for  children.   This   may   be done   in one - off transaction s   with   selected   vendors   o r   through   long - term  arr angements with preferred vendors.   During the   period from 1 January 2022 to 31   October   2023,  UNICEF   spent   a total of US$1 . 798   b i llion   on   institutional service contracts.   In   202 3 , institutional  service contracts   represented   10   per   cent   of   UNICEF’s   global expenditure .   The re were   several  risks a ssociated with the   procurement   and management   of   institutional   service contract s   by  individual office s . I n view of UNICEF’s decentralized structure   and   often   challenging   country  contexts ,   the effectiveness and efficiency of   higher - level governance and oversight   controls were  of particular importan ce . The audit   therefore   sought to determine whether and how UNICEF  managed those risks.  Overall Conclusion  Based   on   the   audit   work   performed,   OIAI  concluded that the assessed governance, risk  management   and   control   processes   were  Partially Satisfactory, Improvement Needed,  meaning   th ey   were   generally   adequate   and  functioning, but needed some improvement. AUDIT   OBJECTIVES ,   SCOPE   AND APPROACH  10  The overarching objective of the audit was to assess the adequacy and effectiveness of the  governance, risk management   and   control processes over a selection of significant risk   areas  related to institutional service contract s   (ISC)   in UNICEF. Th e audit was conducted in two phases .  The first phase consisted of a review of service contracting at the   country office level   during the  regular country office 4   engagements included in OIAI ’ s workplan   in the first   half of   2023.   Those  country offices were selected based on 18 different risk criteria   without specific reference to the  volume or nature of service contract activities. The extent of audit work related to   ISC   was  determined by an initial assessment of all   potential risk areas   during the planning phase of each  audit and varied according to the specific context . 5   However, each audit   sought to determine  whether   processes were designed to ensure that :  •   L ocally contracted services supported programmatic needs and reflected integrity and  transparency in the use of UNICEF funds ; and  •   S ervices (whether procured locally or by S upply   D ivision ) were of the right quality and  quantity and were delivered on time to support efficient and effective delivery of the country  office   programme.  The   audit observations   and actions agreed with the respective   office   management   we re included  in   the   individual   country office   audit report s .   All   o bservations were analysed to identify any  recurring control weaknesses that might indicate systemic issues or   positive practices for potential  replication in other offices. Any significant issues   identified   were   then   to be incorporated in the  present report.  The second phase co vered service contract activities conducted by UNICEF   h eadquarters   and  regional offices   and   sought to determi ne   the adequacy   of design   and   operating   effectiveness of  key controls with respect to   the :  •   Structures and accountabilities in place at   h eadquarters   and   regional office   level s   enabling  efficient and effective use of ISCs across UNICEF ;  •   Mechanisms for overseeing and monitoring ISC s   across UNICEF and identifying and  addressing risks and gaps ;  •   Mechanisms for ensuring effective due diligence of high - value service contracts, including  the Contract Review Committee ,   Suppl ier   Evaluation Unit   and V endor   R eview   C ommittee ;  •   Use of   long - term arrangements   for achieving efficiency in contracting and value - for - money  institutional services   across UNICEF ; and  •   Operational processing of ISCs   requisitioned by   h eadquarters   and regional offices ,  includ ing   assessment of   planning, vendor registration and management, s ourcing and  selection, contract ing ,   contract management   and vendor assessment. Vendor registration and evaluation   High  The audit   identified   the   need   to   enhance   the   efficiency   and effectiveness   of   vendor   registration  and evaluation processes   to mi tigate the risk of   awarding   contracts to vendors   that   are not  financially or ethically sound .  UNGM registration:   The United Nations Global Marketplace (UNGM), the common procurement  portal of U nited   N ations   organizations, provides central screening of vendor eligibility and is  designed to flag any adverse vendor performance experienced by participating agencies.   T he  inter - agency Model Policy Framework   7   (MPF) for vendor sanctioning , being developed with  UNICEF’s participation ,   considers   vendor r egistration   on the UNGM   to be   an   important   control.  While   UNICEF’s procedure on   V endor   R egistration and   M anagement strongly recommends that  all vendors register with the UNGM prior to   seeking   a contractual agreement   with UNICEF ,   and  the related S tandard   O perating   P rocedure (SOP)   includes specific steps to support this,   Supply  Division confirmed to OIAI that   this   is   not mandatory .   The audit team   found that ,   during the audited  period,   out of a total of 1,210 vendors, 152 vendors, with contracts amounting to US$24 million,  were   not registered   in the UNGM .   UNICEF was therefore not   benefitting fully from the   efficiencies  and   information   offered by   this tool   to mitigate   key procurement risks .  Vendor   evaluation:   UNICEF’s   central   Supplier   Evaluation   Unit   (SEU)   is   responsible   for  conduct ing   independent   financial and   ethical   evaluations of   proposed   contracts   that exceed  defined   thresholds ,   to   ensure that the vendor has the necessary financial resources and stability  to deliver the   services and to   identify any indicators of possible   involvement in   fraud, corruption  or other   proscribed practices ,   which mi ght   result in   inefficient , inappropriate   use   o f   funds   or  reputational damage to UNICEF.   According to the Procedure on Vendor Registration and  Management ,   in the event that   a vendor   fails the   SEU   evaluation ,   the SEU will inform the buyer  of the outcome,   request that the vendor be blocked in VISION, and in the case of a failed ethical  evaluation,   refer the case to the V endor Review Committee   (VRC) .   The VRC reviews the case  and   recommends   appropriate remedial measures, taking into consideration programme criticality  and risks facing the organisation. ( S ee Observation Two below .) The SEU evaluation is triggered by a buyer’s request in the Vendor  Evaluation Requests Application (VERA) portal.   The financial evaluation   is   valid for up to  three   years   but   should be re - performed   if there is any change in the vendor’s financial  situation. The Financial Disclosure Policy holds heads of offices accountable for the identification of staff  required to   participat e   in the Financial Disclosure Program me , in addition to those in the  management category.
