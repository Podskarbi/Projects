EXECUTIVE SUMMARY  3  The Office of Internal Audit and Investigations (OIAI) conducted an audit of the   Algeria  Country Office   (ACO) , covering the period from   1 January   2024   to   6 March 2025 .   The audit  was conducted   from   23   February   to   6 March 2025   in   conformance   with   the Institute of  Internal Auditors’ Global   Interna l Audit   Standards.   The objective of the audit was to   assess  the adequacy and effectiveness of   the   governance , risk management ,   and control processes  across   a selection of significant risk areas of the   c ountry office, including   the   control  environment,   programme   management,   cash   transfers   to   implementing   partners ,  including   the   harmonized approach to cash transfer s (HACT)   assurance activities ,   and   the  procurement process .  The   specific   risks evaluated are   set out   in the Audit Objective, Scope and Approach   s ection  of this report .  In 2024, ACO utilized US$7.3 million for   six   outcomes and 23 outputs. The majority of  programme expenditure w as   allocated to child health and nutrition (42   per cent ), education  and adolescent development (26   per cent ), child protection (6   per cent ), social policy (2   per  cent ), programme effectiveness (16   per cent ) and operation al   effectiveness (9   per cent ).  Overall Conclusion  Based   on   the   audit   work   performed,   OIAI  concluded that the assessed governance, risk  management,   or   control   processes   were  generally   adequate   and   functioning   but  needed   improvement.   The   weaknesses   or  deficiencies identified were unlikely to have a  materially   negative   impact   on   the  performance   of   the   audited   entity,   area,  activity or process. OBSERVATIONS AND MANAGEMENT   ACTION PLAN  11  The key areas where actions are needed are summarized below.  1.   Risk   m anagement   Medium  The Algeria Country Office's risk management process could be improved by ensuring key  risks affecting programmatic results for children are identified and addressed, and by  ensuring the efficiency of   c ontrols   to manage them.  The 2023 – 2027 Algeria Country Programme Document mandates regular risk analysis and  mitigation for programmatic and operational risks to achieve planned results and maintain  operational effectiveness. While efforts have been made to integrate risk manageme nt into  strategic planning, further attention may be required to ensure that strategic and emerging  risks are clearly defined and linked to appropriate mitigation measures.  ACO has established a mechanism to identify key risk priorities. However, the process for  including   these   was not documented or validated for accuracy   or   completeness. For  example, the audit noted that the register included nine out of 18 risks identified in the  2023 - 2024 Annual Management Plan (AMP). The remaining AMP risks were excluded from  the register without documented rationale.   ACO   explained to the audit team that these risks  were assessed as low priority and monitored through regular internal revie ws.  The 2024   ACO   risk register included risk analysis, providing risk likelihood, impact, and  residual risks. In most cases reviewed, controls were rated as partially effective, and in two  instances, the controls identified in the annual risk matrix were not adequately des cribed.  Partially effective controls may indicate that proposed mitigation measures do not address  all the risks they are designed to mitigate.   ACO   acknowledged the need to strengthen staff  knowledge   on   risk management to   ensure   a holistic vie w of risks and make informed  decisions related to cash transfers. In   response, OIAI conducted a risk management  workshop to increase staff awareness, with staff expressing a willingness to improve their  knowledge.  Root   causes(s):   S taff   knowledge   in   risk   management   needs   to   be   strengthened   to  effectively   identify, validate   monitor   and   report   key   risks and   emerging risks . 25  APPENDIX  Definition s   of Audit   Observation   Ratings  To assist management in prioritizing the actions arising from the audit, OIAI ascribes a  rating to each audit observation based on the potential consequence or residual risks to  the audited entity, area, activity or   process   or   to   UNICEF as a whole. High  The observation   concerns a   fundamental weakness or deficiency in the  assessed   governance,   risk   management   or   control   processes   that  requires prompt/immediate resolution to avoid severe/major adverse  consequences for the audited entity, area, activity or process ,   or for  UNICEF as a whole.
